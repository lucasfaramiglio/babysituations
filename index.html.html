Parenting Challenge Cards

Click on a number card to reveal a parenting situation.
Think about how you would handle it, then click "See Solution" for suggestions!

1
"Your child throws a tantrum in the supermarket because you won't buy candy."
Suggested approach: Stay calm, kneel to their level, and acknowledge their feelings: "I see you're upset because you want candy. We're not getting candy today, but you can help me choose fruit." Offer limited choices to regain control.
2
"Your toddler refuses to share toys with a playmate."
Suggested approach: Say, "It's hard to share sometimes. Let's take turns - you play for 5 minutes, then your friend gets a turn." Use a timer. Praise sharing behavior when it occurs.
3
"Your 8-year-old is afraid to sleep alone after watching a scary movie."
Suggested approach: Validate the fear: "That movie was scary, wasn't it?" Create a "brave spray" (water in a spray bottle) to "ward off monsters." Do a "monster check" together and gradually reduce reassurance.
4
"Your teenager slams their door after you say they can't go to a party."
Suggested approach: Wait until calm, then say: "I understand you're angry. Let's talk when you're ready." Later, explain your reasons calmly and discuss alternative social plans. Set consequences for door-slamming.
5
"Your 4-year-old refuses to brush their teeth at bedtime."
Suggested approach: Make it fun: "Let's brush the sugar bugs away!" Use a sticker chart. Sing a silly brushing song. Offer limited choices: "Do you want the red or blue toothbrush tonight?"
6
"Your child says 'I hate you!' when you enforce screen time limits."
Suggested approach: Respond calmly: "I know you're upset, but I love you too much to argue." Later, discuss appropriate ways to express anger. Consistently enforce time limits with visual timers.
7
"Your child is crying because their ice cream fell on the ground."
Suggested approach: Empathize first: "Oh no! That was your favorite part, wasn't it?" Offer comfort, then problem-solve together: "Should we clean up and get a new one, or choose something else?"
8
"Your child refuses to wear their coat in cold weather."
Suggested approach: Offer choices: "Would you rather wear this coat or carry it?" Use natural consequences: "If you're cold without it, you can put it on later." Bring the coat regardless.
